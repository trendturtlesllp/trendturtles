Market Sab Jaanta Hai! Haan, News Se Bhi Pehle

Defence stocks moved before Budget 2025 was even announced. If you traded on the news, you were already late — and here is exactly why.

By Neo & Teo  |  TrendTurtles  ·  7 min read  ·  Published: June 2025

Cause & Effect Crowd & Behaviour Data & Readings
HAL surged 2.38% and BEL jumped 5% the day before Budget 2025 — weeks after both had already been climbing on institutional anticipation. Defence allocation rose 9.53% to Rs 6.81 lakh crore, but by the time Nirmala Sitharaman said the number out loud, the move was done. This is not insider trading. This is price anticipation — the one mechanism that costs retail traders money every Budget, every RBI policy day, every earnings season. Here is what actually happened, why it always happens, and what to do about it.

1. Old Delhi Railway Station, Platform No. 3 — Budget Day, 10:47 AM

The platform smells of chai, coal dust, and impatience. Train: 15 minutes late.

Teo is on the bench. Earphones in. Knee bouncing. Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman is live on his phone screen, walking up to the podium.

He bought Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL) last night. Three Twitter accounts said defence budget badhega. A WhatsApp group had a "CONFIRMED SOURCE." He went in.

Next to him, Neo has Economic & Political Weekly open on his lap. Reading it like someone who already knows how the day ends.

The announcement comes: defence allocation up 9.53%. Teo almost pumps his fist.

He opens his brokerage app.

BEL: down 1.8%.

He refreshes. Still red.

Usi waqt ek coolie red shirt mein aata hai — seedha Teo ke trolley bag ko haath lagata hai.

"Saab, utha doon?"

"Hatt bhai! Aaj sab ne milke loot liya hai mujhe — tu bhi aa gaya?!"
— Teo, to the coolie, phone trembling in his left hand.

The coolie steps back, genuinely confused.

Neo glances up from his magazine. Looks at the coolie. Looks at Teo. Closes the magazine slowly.

"Tune kab liya tha?"
— Neo to Teo, quiet, no expression.
"Kal raat. News aayi thi na — toh socha abhi bhi time hai—"
— Teo to Neo, voice dropping.

Neo picks up Teo's phone. Opens the BEL chart. 3 months.

"Yeh dekh. November se chadh raha tha."
— Neo to Teo, ek finger chart pe.

The chart is simple and brutal. BEL had been climbing for weeks — quietly, steadily. No announcement. No news. Just price, moving up before anyone on Twitter said a word.

Teo stares. The anger does not leave — it just shifts direction.

"Yeh sab pehle se jaante the? Yeh insider trading nahi hai kya?!"
— Teo to Neo, voice still sharp.
"Insider nahi. Anticipation. Bahut fark hai."
— Neo to Teo, magazine uthata hua wapas.

The train pulls in. Neo gestures to the coolie — "Bhai, bag le lo" — and the coolie lifts his bag smoothly and follows. Teo walks behind, phone in hand, still staring at the chart.

Did You Notice?

HAL, BEL, BDL, and GRSE surged 4–9% on January 31 — the day BEFORE Budget 2025. And they had already been climbing for weeks before that. When the 9.53% defence allocation was announced on February 1, most of the move was done. The news confirmed what price had already told you. Early buyers were thinking about their exit — not their entry.

2. What Actually Happened — And Why It Always Happens

Markets do not wait for certainty. They price the probability of an outcome — weeks, sometimes months, in advance.

"Toh matlab market bhavishya dekhta hai?"
— Teo to Neo, compartment ki khidki se bahar dekhte hue.
"Bhavishya nahi. Probability price karta hai. Jitni certainty, utna pehle move."
— Neo to Teo, settle hote hue.

When fund managers, institutions, and systematic traders all arrive at the same conclusion — defence budget will rise, RBI will cut rates — they begin accumulating quietly. That accumulation IS the price move.

By the time CNBC confirms it, the move is over.

"Toh main kya kar raha tha? Unka exit dhoondh raha tha unke liye?"
— Teo to Neo, half-laughing, half-pained.
"Exactly."
— Neo to Teo, ek eyebrow upar.

3. Three Mistakes Every News-Driven Trader Makes

Mistake 1 — Buying the Announcement, Not the Anticipation

The announcement is the finish line, not the starting gun.

HAL, BDL, and BEL have seen India's defence budget grow from Rs 2.49 lakh crore in 2016 to Rs 6.81 lakh crore in 2025 — a near-tripling over a decade, per IGIRF defence budget data. Institutional money tracked this trend year after year. They did not buy on Budget day. They bought on the trend — and Budget day was their exit.

"Toh yeh log budget se pehle hi nikal gaye the?"
— Teo to Neo, genuinely surprised.
"Kuch nikal gaye. Kuch held. Par entry toh November mein thi — February mein nahi."
— Neo to Teo, simply.

Mistake 2 — Trusting Social Proof Over Price Action

When three Twitter accounts, two Telegram groups, and a WhatsApp forward all say the same thing — it feels like signal. It is noise with consensus.

By the time a theme is trending, institutional positioning is done. The correct question is never "what are people saying?" It is "what has price already done?"

"Yaar, toh main akela bewakoof nahi hoon — sab yahi karte hain?"
— Teo to Neo, thodi relief ke saath.
"Sab karte hain. Isliye kaam nahi karta."
— Neo to Teo, halka sa smirk.

Mistake 3 — Mistaking Confirmation for Opportunity

Good news confirmed is a selling opportunity for early buyers — not a buying opportunity for late entrants.

The retail trader who buys on the news is providing exactly the exit liquidity that early money needs. Every time. This is not manipulation. It is just how price discovery works.

4. IF-THEN — When to Actually Act on Anticipated News

The goal is not to avoid news-driven sectors. The goal is to position with anticipation, not after confirmation.

IF a sector theme appears in the Economic Survey or pre-budget ministry statements — and the sectoral index has not yet moved more than 8% from its 60-day base —

THEN consider staggered entry into Mirae Asset Nifty India Defence ETF (NIDEFENCE) over 2–3 weeks before the Budget date.

IF RBI MPC meeting is scheduled and bond yield movement shows 70%+ probability of a rate cut —

THEN watch rate-sensitive NBFCs 10–15 days before the announcement, not after. Track via Nippon India Banking & PSU Debt Fund (NIPPSUGR).

IF a Nifty 50 heavyweight is approaching earnings with analyst consensus showing 15%+ YoY profit growth AND the stock has underperformed its sector by 5%+ in the prior 30 days —

One Last Thing

"Toh aage se main kya karun — news hi nahi dekhun?"
— Teo to Neo, station peeche chhoot gaya, train speed pe.
"News dekh. Bas chart pehle dekh. Price already bata raha hota hai — sun."
— Neo to Teo, magazine wapas kholta hua.

The market is not smarter than you. It is just earlier than you.

Institutions read the same Economic Survey, the same ministry statements, the same global data. But they act on probability — before confirmation. That window, between probability forming and news confirming, is where returns live.

After the announcement, you are not trading information. You are trading other people's exits.

Teo looked out the window at the blur of platforms passing. Somewhere back there, the coolie was probably helping someone else with their luggage. Doing his job. Not his fault.

The phone went face-down on the seat.

Key Takeaways

  1. Markets price the probability of an event weeks before it happens — HAL, BEL, BDL moved 4–9% before Budget 2025 was even announced.
  2. When good news arrives and price falls — that is not a malfunction. The move was already complete.
  3. Social media consensus is a lagging indicator. By the time a trade idea is trending, institutional positioning is done.
  4. The entry window is between probability forming and news confirming — not after the news drops.
  5. Retail traders who buy on announcement day are providing exit liquidity for those who bought weeks earlier. Know which side of that trade you want to be on.
